# COYL — Series A Investor Pipeline

> 30 named investors, three categories. For each: the warm-intro
> path (who introduces you), the angle (what to lead with), and
> the prior portfolio fit.

**Round:** $10M Series A ($8M lead + $2M follow-on)
**Date:** May 2026
**Target close:** 90 days from outreach launch
**Owner:** Iman Schrock + the future Head of BD (Month 3 hire)

---

## How to read this doc

Three categories, ten investors each. The doc is sequenced by
**probability of close**, not by alphabet or by fund size. The
top names in each category are the highest-probability targets;
the bottom names are the backup tier.

The pipeline is **wider than necessary on purpose**. A Series A
raise typically converts at 1–3% of first-meeting to wire. 30
first meetings → 1 lead, with 5–8 secondary checks. The pipeline
needs all 30 to reliably produce the lead.

**Outreach sequencing:** the first ten go in Week 1–2. The
second ten go in Week 3–4 (so the first batch has time to
produce signal before the second batch dilutes attention). The
third ten go in Week 5–6 only if the first 20 haven't produced
a lead.

## Outreach sequencing — the 90-day plan

**Week 1–2:** First 10 names (tier-1 healthtech). Outreach
sequenced by warm-intro density — the highest-density paths go
first to give the warm-intro asks time to mature.

**Week 3–4:** Next 10 names (generalist with health-AI exposure)
if any tier-1 hasn't moved to a second meeting. The signal: a
tier-1 fund that has gone quiet after a first meeting is a
soft pass; activate the generalist tier.

**Week 5–6:** Final 10 names (family offices + strategic
adjacency) only if the prior 20 haven't produced a lead. The
strategic-adjacency tier is the "fill the round + pre-position
the acquisition" layer, not the "lead the round" layer.

**Week 7–9:** Term sheet negotiation + reference checks +
co-investor syndicate building. Lead announces with $2M of the
follow-on tier pre-committed.

**Week 10–12:** Close + wire. Allocation, signing, capital call.

---

## The discipline — three rules

1. **Never two first-meetings in the same week with funds that
   compete for the lead.** Sequoia + a16z + Greylock are direct
   competitors; never run all three in the same week. The
   founder's attention is the limiter.

2. **Always have a backup intro path.** The warm-intro paths in
   this doc each have at least one alternative. If the primary
   path is unavailable in a 2-week window, switch to the
   alternative immediately — don't wait.

3. **The first meeting is for the founder to learn the firm;
   the second meeting is for the firm to commit.** Never expect
   a term sheet from a first meeting. The discipline is "leave
   the room with a clear next step in the next 7 days" — that
   is the only meaningful first-meeting outcome.
