## Trigger conditions

### Trigger conditions for next raise (Series B)

We open the Series B raise at **Month 11** if any of the
following are true:

1. **Consumer ARR ≥ $15M annualized** (the single-tier model
   validated)
2. **1 GLP-1 partner live + 1 signed LOI in flight** (BD lane
   activated)
3. **IRB approved + first patient enrolled** (clinical pathway
   on schedule)
4. **Self-Trust Score 12-month cohort showing ≥35% improvement**
   (the cohort metric per the seed-deck Slide 9)

The Series B target: **$30–50M at $150–250M post**, lead by a
growth-stage healthtech fund (Insight, Tiger, GC growth, or
Bessemer growth) with the original Series A tier participating
pro-rata.

### Trigger conditions for strategic acquisition

We open the strategic-acquisition conversation in parallel at
**Month 12** if any of the following are true:

1. **Series B raise stalled at <$25M committed by Month 11** (the
   market is signaling weakness)
2. **Microsoft + Meta + Apple have each had at least 2 strategic
   conversations** by Month 10 (the FOMO seed has germinated per
   the 6b-action-plan)
3. **The substrate (Layer 1–4) is fully scaffolded** + the
   clinical interim data is positive + 1 GLP-1 partner is live
   (the strategic acquirer has full visibility on the asset)

The strategic-LOI target: **$1.5–4.5B per the 6b-action-plan
Scenario B / C bands**, with the bidder-set explicitly designed
to generate competitive tension between Microsoft (Viva), Meta
(Reality Labs), and Apple (Health).

### Trigger conditions for bridge round

We open a bridge round at **Month 13** if neither the Series B
nor the strategic-LOI path has closed by Month 12. The bridge:

- $3–5M from existing Series A investors + strategic angels
  (Calibrate / Hims / Ro alumni)
- Convertible note OR safe at the next-round price ceiling of
  the Series A post-money ($55M+)
- 12-month runway extension to test the next milestone

The bridge is the "we got close but didn't close, give us 12
more months" path. It dilutes more than a clean Series B
($55M-implied price vs. $150M+ Series B post) but is structurally
preferable to a down round.

## The deal terms

For the prospective lead's reference.

| Term | Standard | COYL Series A |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-money | varies | $45M target |
| Round size | $5–15M Series A | $10M |
| Liquidation preference | 1× non-participating | 1× non-participating |
| Anti-dilution | broad-based weighted average | broad-based weighted average |
| Pay-to-play | optional | yes, with 50% threshold |
| Pro-rata rights | yes (lead + meaningful syndicate) | yes |
| Board | 1 investor + 1 founder + 1 independent | 2 founder + 1 investor + 2 independent (target) |
| Information rights | major investor only | quarterly to all; monthly to lead |
| Drag-along | majority of common | yes |
| Co-sale rights | yes | yes |
| ROFR | yes | yes |
| Founder vesting | 4 years, 1 year cliff | 4 years from original start date; 75% vested at close |
| Founder acceleration | single-trigger on COC if terminated | single-trigger on COC if terminated within 12 months |

**The single non-standard term:** the founder is **75% vested at
close** because the founder started the company 18+ months ago
and has been the sole engineer + sole executor through the
pre-Series-A phase. A 1-year cliff at Series A would reset the
vesting clock; the 75% vested-at-close is the negotiating ask
that lands the right founder commitment without rewarding the
investor for being late to the company.

**Counsel:** Cooley or Fenwick (the founder's choice; pick before
the term sheet negotiation opens).

## Use of proceeds — milestone gates

The capital releases against milestones, not against time. The
$10M is wired at close; the **operating gates** below structure
the deployment internally.

| Gate | Trigger | Capital unlock |
|---|---|---|
| Gate 1 | Close + first board meeting | Engineering hires 1+2, founder salary, legal/ops base |
| Gate 2 | Engineer 1+2 onboarded, CTO offer accepted | CTO start, engineer 3 search, Head of Clinical search |
| Gate 3 | CTO + Head of Clinical onboarded | Head of BD search, paid acquisition build-out |
| Gate 4 | Found Health signed, IRB submitted | Paid acquisition turns on at $80K/mo cap, ML eng start |
| Gate 5 | First IRB-approved patient enrolled | Conference budget releases, Q-Sub prep starts |
| Gate 6 | Q-Sub teleconference complete | Head of Growth or Compliance start, second cohort enrollment |
| Gate 7 | Consumer ARR $8M annualized | Series B raise opens, BD spend doubles |
| Gate 8 | Series B / Strategic LOI close | $10M deployed, transition to Series B operating plan |

The gates are not gates against the cash (the cash flows on the
burn schedule). The gates are **decision moments** — at each gate
the founder + board confirm the next bucket releases as planned
or trims per the if-we-miss tree in capital-allocation §6.
