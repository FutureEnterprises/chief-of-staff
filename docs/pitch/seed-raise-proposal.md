## §1 — The recommendation

| Term | Value |
|---|---|
| **Raise amount** | **$2.5M** |
| **Instrument** | Post-money SAFE (YC standard) with MFN |
| **Valuation cap** | **$15M post-money** |
| **Discount** | None (cap-only) |
| **Pro-rata rights** | Yes, for any check ≥ $250K |
| **Target close** | 90 days from launch · soft-circle in 45 |
| **Lead check** | $1M – $1.5M |
| **Syndicate** | 3 – 6 operator / strategic angels at $50K – $300K |

### Why this number, in one paragraph

The substrate is real (protocol stack shipped, consumer site live,
billing wired, clinical protocol drafted). The revenue is not (no
paying users yet). The team is one person (recruiting technical
cofounder + clinical advisor). At this honest stage, **$2.5M at a $15M
post-money cap is the band where the raise is closeable in 90 days,
the dilution is reasonable (~14% before option pool top-up), and the
milestones the cap underwrites — first 1,000 paying users + IRB
submission + Viva commercial conversation — are actually deliverable
in 18 months.** A higher cap would be unsupported by the absence of
revenue; a lower cap would over-dilute the founder before the
technical cofounder is in.

### Justification, against the strategy doc

The internal `docs/strategy/strategy-v3.md` and `docs/pitch/seed-deck.md`
(prior version) modeled a **$4–6M raise at $20–30M pre.** That sizing
assumed a team already hired and a consumer ARR ramp already running.
**Neither assumption is true today.** The honest seed sizing is
smaller, the cap is lower, and the milestones the round buys are the
ones that justify the next raise — not the ones that pretend the next
raise already happened.

When the prescriber wedge produces the first 1,000 paying users + the
Viva conversation reaches a commercial paragraph + the IRB is
submitted, **a priced seed extension or full Series A at $20–40M
pre-money is the natural next step.** That is the optionality the SAFE
cap preserves.

## §2 — Lead-investor profile

The lead has to fit on two axes simultaneously: **consumer health**
(GLP-1 / behavior change / clinical pathway) and **AI infrastructure**
(protocol-layer thinking, open-spec strategy, OEM channel). Most funds
pick one. The right lead picks both.

### Dual-fit thesis (what the lead believes before pitch)

1. **Behavior change is a consumer-health category that finally has
   a technology substrate.** The lead is looking for the "Stripe of
   behavior change" — a horizontal layer other apps build on, not a
   vertical brand that competes inside a crowded category.
2. **GLP-1s exposed the gap, and the gap is structural.** The lead
   understands the Cambridge 60% regain number and that the
   manufacturers cannot fix it pharmacologically.
3. **Open protocols are how new categories anchor.** The lead has seen
   the MCP / Anthropic open-spec model and recognizes that the Apache
   2.0 protocol stack is the strategic asset, not the consumer app.
4. **Founder-led is a feature, not a bug, at this stage.** The lead
   underwrites the founder's velocity from the existing surface and
   actively wants to be in the technical-cofounder recruiting conversation.

### Negative signals (founder walks away)

- "We need to see ARR before we engage."  — wrong stage of fund.
- "Can you launch in 90 days as a B2B-only company?"  — wrong thesis.
- "We'd want to lead a $5M+ round."  — wrong size for this milestone.
- "We pass on solo founders."  — wrong category understanding.

### Thesis-fit investor targets (by profile, not name)

> _path matters more than the name. The founder runs targeting against_
> _these profiles with the M&A advisor + warm-intro network._

| Profile | Why they fit | Example shape |
|---|---|---|
| **Bio-meets-consumer fund** | Underwrite clinical-grade consumer apps; pattern-match against Devoted Health / Insitro / Headway | a16z Bio · GV health · Section 32 |
| **Consumer health seed specialist** | Understand subscription psychology + GLP-1 category dynamics | Forerunner · Lightspeed Consumer · Maveron |
| **AI-infrastructure-first fund** | Open-spec + protocol-layer thinking; understand the MCP analogy | Conviction · Felicis · Initialized · Spark |

The realistic best-case lead is a **bio-meets-consumer partner who

## §3 — 18-month milestone plan (tied to tranches)

The $2.5M deploys against three milestone tranches. The tranches are
internal operating gates, not legal release conditions on the SAFE.

### Tranche 1 — Months 1–3 · ~$500K

> _Stand up the inbound channel. First paying-user cohort. Prove the_
> _prescriber wedge converts._

**Deliverables**
- Technical cofounder offer signed + onboarded (Month 1)
- Clinical advisor identified + LOI signed (Month 2)
- First engineer hired (Month 3)
- Prescriber cold-outreach activated — 50 DMs, 10+ replies, 3+ pilot clinics live
- First 100 paying users on the consumer surface (Rebound + Rewire combined)
- Billing instrumented end-to-end · Stripe webhook → cohort dashboard

**Gate to Tranche 2**
First 100 paying users live. Technical cofounder onboarded. Clinical
advisor LOI signed.

### Tranche 2 — Months 4–9 · ~$1.0M

> _Manufacture the prescriber proof. Get the Viva conversation into a_
> _commercial paragraph._

**Deliverables**
- 1,000 paying users (consumer + prescriber-channel combined)
- 5+ active prescriber pilot clinics with >25 patients each
- Microsoft Viva partner-program accepted; commercial conversation opened
- IRB submission filed at the partner clinical site
- Self-Trust Score cohort dashboard live; first published cohort note
- Designer hired · second engineer offer extended
- First analyst / press hit on the protocol-as-Switzerland framing

**Gate to Tranche 3**
1,000 paying users live. Viva commercial conversation has a stated
shape (pilot scope, signal data exchange). IRB filed.

### Tranche 3 — Months 10–18 · ~$1.0M

> _Position for the priced seed extension or Series A._

**Deliverables**
- 5,000+ paying users with 50%+ 6-month retention validated
- 1 GLP-1 telehealth partner live (Found / Ro Body / Calibrate / Sequence)
- Viva pilot agreement signed (paid or co-funded)
- IRB-approved RCT enrolling
- 2nd protocol implementer announced (the "Switzerland" signal)
- Priced seed extension or Series A opened at Month 15

**Exit gate from Seed**
- $1M+ ARR run-rate
- 1 enterprise OEM contract signed (Viva or adjacency)
- Clinical RCT enrolling against a published protocol with named PI

If all three gates land, **the next raise opens at $20–40M pre on
priced terms with the same lead leading or co-leading the upround.**
If two of three land, the SAFE extension at the same $15M cap remains
the structural insurance. If fewer than two land, the founder
re-underwrites the model rather than raising flat.

## §5 — Why a SAFE, not a priced seed

Three reasons the SAFE is the right structure right now.

1. **Speed.** A priced seed adds 4–6 weeks of board / governance
   negotiation. A SAFE closes when the lead signs.
2. **Cap preservation.** A priced seed at $15M post forces the
   founder to defend the cap against the absence of revenue. A SAFE
   cap is a ceiling on the next priced round, not a stated valuation
   today — which is the honest position at this stage.
3. **Optionality for the priced upround.** If the 18-month milestones
   land, the next raise is a priced seed extension or Series A at a
   genuinely higher valuation. The SAFE converts cleanly into that
   round; a priced seed today would anchor the upround lower.

The only investor type that pushes back on a SAFE at this stage is a
fund whose LPA requires a priced round. **For that case the founder
offers a $2.5M priced seed at $12.5M pre / $15M post with the same
substantive economics.** No anti-dilution, no participation, no
liquidation preference above 1× non-participating. The structure is
the same outcome with more legal expense.

### The three risks an investor will probably weigh hardest

These are the ones to pressure-test in the founder's own head before
pitching.

1. **The solo-founder risk** (row 1). Naming the technical cofounder
   recruit as the first dollar deployed is the strongest pre-empt.
2. **The clinical-path risk** (rows 2 + 7). The clinical study is
   pathway-mapped, not enrolled; the 60% number is published, not
   self-generated. Don't oversell either.
3. **The strategic-channel risk** (rows 3 + 4). Both the prescriber
   wedge and the Viva conversation are real but unproven. Tranche 1
   exists specifically to convert one of them into proof.
