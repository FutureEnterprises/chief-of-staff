# Round-3 audit — three founder-only action items

*Saved May 30, 2026. From the external Round-3 review. These three
items are work the founder personally has to do — they cannot be
delegated to Claude, an engineer, an agency, or a hire. Each one is
load-bearing on the strategic exit path the audit outlined.*

---

## 1. Maximize Found Health RCT enrollment

**Why this is #1**: the auditor identified — correctly — that the Found
Health RCT is the single thing that turns COYL from "Claude wrapper
with copy" into "behavioral health company with publishable evidence."
If the slip-recovery rate in the intervention arm is 20%+ better than
control, you have a deck slide that Novo Nordisk has to acknowledge. If
the study under-enrolls or runs out of statistical power, you don't.

**What only the founder can do:**

- Call Found Health's clinical operations lead this week. Not their
  comms team. The clinical ops lead is the person who controls patient
  recruitment into the study.
- Offer COYL **free to ALL Found Health GLP-1 patients during the
  study window**, not just the enrolled arm. This expands the enrolled
  population and creates a soft control group from the patients who
  use it without consenting to formal study participation.
- Personally commit to weekly enrollment sync calls with their team
  through the primary endpoint readout. Not "we'll check in" —
  a calendared 30-min standing meeting.
- Get on a plane and meet their clinical lead in person before
  Month 2. The relationship needs to be founder-to-founder strong
  before the data lands.

**How you'll know it's done**: enrolled-N is ≥120 by Month 3, with
≥100 days of intervention data captured per patient. Below that and
the primary endpoint won't have statistical power.

## 2. Publish the personal GLP-1 journey essay

**Why this is #2**: the auditor's harshest finding was "anonymous +
no proprietary tech + no users = this is a landing page, not a
company." The founder coming forward is the single highest-leverage
move because it inverts the trust calculation in seconds.

The essay the auditor suggested — *"I lost 60 pounds on Wegovy,
regained 40, and built COYL because I realized the shot doesn't train
the pattern"* — is the right shape. Don't undersell the vulnerability.
The reason COYL works as a thesis is because the founder is the user.
Say so on the record.

**What only the founder can do:**

- Write the essay in your own voice. Not in mine, not in any
  ghostwriter's. The credibility of the piece is exclusively that
  YOU wrote it.
- Include the actual numbers: weight lost, weight regained, the
  specific 9 PM script that broke you, the specific moment you
  realized this was a class of problem and not a personal failing.
- Publish on Substack first. Cross-post to LinkedIn 2 hours later.
  Pin it on your X profile. Submit to STAT News + TechCrunch +
  Hard Fork as the founder-story behind the COYL launch.
- Update `/about` on coyl.ai to lead with the essay's framing,
  not the current third-person bio.
- Remove "GCT (NASDAQ: GCT)" from the COYL author line until
  GCT's General Counsel has cleared the outside CEO role
  (v3 plan, Month 1 gating step). Anonymous-founder mode is
  worse than named-founder mode, but named-founder mode without
  GCT board approval is a Form 4 / Reg FD problem. Do one before
  the other.

**How you'll know it's done**: 5,000+ Substack reads in the first
two weeks, at least one inbound from a healthcare VC referencing
the essay specifically, and the `/about` page leads with the
founder narrative instead of corporate language.

## 3. File 3 provisional patents on the clinical protocol

**Why this is #3**: the auditor correctly observed there is no
proprietary technology to patent — COYL is a Claude wrapper. But
there ARE patentable METHOD claims around the behavioral protocol
itself. Specifically:

- **Archetype-based danger-window prediction**: the method of
  classifying users into one of N behavioral families and using
  that classification to generate per-user danger-window
  schedules from sensor + interaction data.
- **Same-night recovery protocol**: the method of intervening
  within a specific time window after a self-reported slip with
  archetype-specific scripts to interrupt the cascade.
- **Excuse-taxonomy classification**: the method of categorizing
  user-uttered or detected internal-justification phrases into
  a finite taxonomy and using that taxonomy to select
  interventions.

**What only the founder can do:**

- Engage a healthcare-experienced patent attorney (not a generalist).
  Recommend: someone from Wilson Sonsini's life-sciences IP group,
  Cooley's digital-health IP team, or boutiques like Bookoff
  McAndrews. The choice of attorney matters more than the spend.
- Personally walk through the protocol with them. The method claims
  have to be PRECISELY described. A generalist will draft them too
  broadly and §101 will eat them; a specialist will draft them at
  the right level of abstraction.
- Provisional patent filings cost ~$3K-5K each. Budget $15K for
  three filings. This comes out of the pre-seed, not founder cash.
- File before any clinical RCT publication. Once you publish,
  one-year statutory bar starts. Don't run out the clock.

**A real-talk note**: behavioral health method patents are
fragile under current §101 jurisprudence (Mayo, Alice). They are
worth filing for defensive value and as a signal to acquirers,
but do not let your fundraise narrative depend on them. The actual
moat is the data + brand + Found Health relationship. The patents
are insurance.

**How you'll know it's done**: three provisional applications
filed with USPTO with PTAS receipts by Month 4. Non-provisional
conversions scheduled for Month 14-16 (within the 12-month
statutory window).

  ongoing pitch hygiene, not a discrete TODO. Bake it into every
  investor conversation going forward.

The three items above are the founder-only actions that have to
happen in parallel during Months 1-4 of the v3 plan. They are not
sequential — start all three this week.
