## Section 3 — The 20 hardest questions, with answers

The founder will be asked these. The answers are the founder's
voice; quote freely.

### 1. Isn't this just a smart journal?

No. A journal is what you write after the moment. COYL is what
fires _in_ the moment. The architecture is four layers — Detect
(passive signal stack), Predict (per-user model), Fire (state-
matched intervention with confidence gating), Resolve (recovery
flow if the user slips). A journal occupies one of those layers
(Detect, retrospectively). COYL occupies all four, in real time.

### 2. Doesn't this just track me?

No more than your Apple Watch already does. The privacy architecture
is the read: on-device inference for the per-user prediction (no
raw HRV samples leave the device); BAA-covered cloud for the
population model and outcome data (HIPAA Safe Harbor de-
identification); full data export; right to deletion; no third-
party advertising SDKs. The product never had to choose between
privacy and accuracy because the per-user model runs locally.

### 3. What if you fire interrupts at the wrong moment?

The model is confidence-gated. Below a 0.62 confidence threshold,
the engine defers rather than fires. When the user tags a fire as
"wrong moment," the per-user model learns from the negative case.
False-positive rate in the V0.1 internal beta is below 40% in
week 2; the target for V0.2 GA is below 25%. The product is
designed to under-fire rather than over-fire. The user can
disable individual firing channels in the settings.

### 4. Does this actually work?

The 12-week randomized study is in setup with a partner clinic.
Primary endpoint is differential weight regain at +90 days post
GLP-1 discontinuation; N = 80; pre-registered SAP; IRB-expedited
pathway. Interim feasibility data from the pilot cohort will be
available before the V0.2 App Store submission. We are
deliberately not making efficacy claims in advance of the
peer-reviewed readout. The product is a behavioral support tool,
not a medical device. The full protocol is at coyl.ai/clinical-study.

### 5. Why are you better than Noom?

Noom is content + coaching, delivered asynchronously. COYL is a
real-time pattern interrupt, delivered synchronously to the moment
the behavior is about to happen. The product surfaces are
architecturally different — Noom's surface is a 20-minute coaching
session in the afternoon; COYL's surface is a Live Activity that
fires on the lock screen at 9:47 PM. Noom's engagement metric is
session length; COYL's engagement metric is interrupt success rate.
Different categories. We don't compete with Noom for the same user
moment.

### 6. What's stopping Apple from building this?

Three things. First, the patent stack — provisional applications
filed on the per-user prediction loop and the lock-screen Live
Activity intervention surface (see `docs/ip/` for current state).
Second, the behavioral dataset — by the time Apple notices, COYL
will have 100K+ users worth of slip events, each one keyed to an
archetype × danger-window × resolution outcome. A new entrant with
5K users cannot replicate that model. Third, the category brand —
in the same way "search" became Google and "messaging" became
iMessage, "pattern interrupt" becomes the product that owned the
moment first.

### 7. Are you selling to Big Pharma?

No. We are not running an M&A process. The strategic-acquisition
multiple is named honestly because every investor and journalist
will ask about it. The strategic-asset path is real (pharma
adjacency, payer multipliers, behavioral data licensing) and we
will not pretend otherwise. The product is built to be valuable
as a standalone consumer + enterprise + clinical business; the
strategic premium is upside, not the thesis.

### 8. What about people who don't have an Apple Watch?

Graceful degradation. The product works on iPhone alone — passive
signals available without a Watch are screen-on patterns,
sedentary duration (via Motion & Fitness), location context (with
consent), and calendar context. The Watch adds HRV and sleep
fidelity but is not required. Android parity ships V0.3 via
Health Connect + Wear OS. Web fallback is the lowest-fidelity path
and is still useful — danger-window pushes via Web Push work on
any browser.

### 9. What data do you collect?

The minimum required for the prediction to work. Behavioral events
(slip events, recovery events, interrupt resolutions), the user's
audit responses (archetype + danger window mapping), passive
HealthKit signals processed locally on-device. PHI is BAA-covered,
HIPAA Safe Harbor de-identified before any model training. The
user can export everything, delete everything, and re-consent to
each new data type per the consent architecture. GDPR-compliant
for EU users. No third-party advertising SDKs. The full data
practices are at coyl.ai/privacy.

### 10. What if the AI is wrong?

The user can ignore the interrupt — no penalty, no shame. The user
can tag the fire as "wrong moment," and the per-user model learns
from the negative case. The user can disable individual firing
channels. The product is designed to fail toward inaction, not
toward harassment. We measure false-positive rate continuously and
target sub-25% at V0.2 GA.

### 11. Why open-source the protocol?

Category-shaping. The fastest path to universal adoption of a new
behavioral category is to let anyone implement the protocol. BIP
v0.1 is Apache 2.0; the spec, the conformance suite, and the
reference engine are public. COYL Cloud is the paid reference
implementation. Other products can implement BIP and be conformance-
tested against COYL Cloud. The category wins together; we win
because we built the engine first and we keep the data flywheel.
The play is the same as MCP for software systems: open spec, paid
reference engine, category control.

### 12. Are you trying to replace therapy?

No. Adjunctive, not substitutive. The product surfaces the 988
crisis line + NEDA helpline + the partner clinic's referral path
in the safety banner whenever crisis-adjacent content is detected.
Therapy is for the long arc — the patterns and the meanings. COYL
is for the moment — the three seconds before action. They are
complementary; the user can do both. Our clinical board includes
practicing therapists for exactly this reason.

### 13. Why charge for it if it's open-source?

The spec is open. The reference engine is paid. The app and the
per-user model and the Live Activity intervention surface are
paid. We made it deliberate: anyone can implement BIP and ship a
competing product. We win because we built the engine first, we
have the data flywheel, and we are the conformance test target.
Competing products on the protocol are good for us — the category
grows; we are the reference; the dataset compounds.

The founder is Iman Schrock — shipping daily since the product
began. Seed-funded hires (first 90 days): senior full-stack
engineer, growth lead, BD lead with telehealth Rx Rolodex, clinical
PI (advisor or fractional), designer. The advisory board is being
formed; the clinical board is in active recruitment with an

### 15. How are you raising?

A $4–6M Seed round is active at $20–30M pre-money. Series A is
projected at $18–22M raised at $80–130M pre, Month 12–15, against
$7–9M ARR + one GLP-1 partner live + RCT 12-week data in hand. The
Series A is deliberately oversized to survive the 24–30 month
payer-channel dead zone that bankrupted Pear Therapeutics. Series
B is projected at $40–60M, Month 30–36, against $40M+ ARR and FDA
filing in review. The full capital plan is at coyl.ai/strategy
(internal; available to senior journalists under NDA).

### 16. What's the GLP-1 angle?

The GLP-1 maintenance problem is the wedge. 10M+ US patients on
semaglutide / tirzepatide / liraglutide; 60–80% regain weight
within two years of discontinuation; the drug suppresses appetite
chemically but does not modify the learned behavioral patterns
that drove pre-treatment overeating. The medicated window is a
unique training opportunity: the patient has enough appetite
suppression to practice alternative responses to historical
trigger contexts without the override pressure of acute hunger.
COYL is the behavioral context layer for that window. The
clinical RCT is testing this hypothesis directly.

### 17. How big is the market?

The English-speaking addressable market today is 180–250M users —
the intersection of measurable autopilot-driven cost, smartphone
access, and willingness to pay. The global ten-year market is
600–800M. The category-specific markets we sit adjacent to: AI
mental health (projected $31.66B by 2035, Precedence Research),
behavioral health digital therapeutics, EAP services ($5.1B US
with 3–6% utilization), GLP-1 maintenance ($50B+ adjacent),
habit-tracking and productivity ($2B+), and therapy ($35B US).
We do not lead with the bigger number. The defensible TAM is the
$12B+ already being spent on solutions that intervene after the
moment.

### 18. What happens if a user is in crisis?

The product surfaces the 988 crisis line + NEDA helpline + the
partner clinic referral path whenever crisis-adjacent content is
detected in the in-app text. The PI on the clinical study is
notified within 24 hours via de-identified flag (no content is
shared off-device). The clinical board reviews every flagged
event quarterly. The safety architecture is mandatory; it is not
behind a paywall. The full safety policy is at coyl.ai/safety.

### 19. Aren't you just another wellness app?

No. Wellness apps occupy the content-and-coaching surface (Noom,
Calm, Headspace, BetterHelp). COYL occupies the pattern-interrupt
surface, which is architecturally different. Wellness apps measure
session length; COYL measures interrupt success rate. Wellness
apps run asynchronously; COYL runs synchronously to the moment of
behavior. Wellness apps are an asynchronous category we are
adjacent to but do not compete with for the same user moment.
We're a different category. The pattern interrupt is the category;
the wellness app is not.

### 20. What's the 12-month roadmap?

Phase 1 (Month 0–3): V0.2 ship — Live Activity, Watch
complication, ActivityKit + App Intents. App Store Featured
nomination. Seed close. First API LOI signed (sobriety partner).
Phase 2 (Month 3–6): RCT enrollment active; IRB approved.
Enterprise pilots — 5 logos at $3–5 PEPM. First press cycle

Phase 4 (Month 9–12): RCT follow-up + 12-week readout. Series A

## Section 6 — Embargo, exclusivity, and cadence

The discipline that protects both the journalist and the company.

### Embargo offer

- **Standard embargo: 72 hours pre-publish.** The journalist
  receives the kit + the founder interview + any requested assets
  72 hours before the embargo lifts. The founder will not comment
  to any other outlet during the embargo window.
- **Embargo break clause:** if the embargo is broken by another
  outlet, the journalist is released from the embargo at the same
  moment.
- **Embargo formality:** the embargo is named explicitly in the
  pitch email and confirmed in writing before the founder
  interview. A gentleman's agreement is not an embargo.

### Exclusivity

- **One-outlet exclusive: 7-day window** offered to one tier-1
  outlet per phase. Tier-1 candidates: Bloomberg, NYT, The Verge,
  Wired, WSJ, MIT Technology Review.
- **The exclusive is the launch story.** All subsequent press is
  released the day the exclusive publishes, on coordinated
  timing.
- **Reasons we'd offer an exclusive:** a tier-1 outlet commits to a
  feature-length piece with the founder as primary subject; the
  outlet has a strategic distribution multiplier on our category;
  the journalist has demonstrated track record on adjacent stories.
- **No two-outlet "soft exclusives."** We will say no to that
  framing. It is either exclusive or it is shared.

### Founder availability

- **30-min interview slots,** scheduled the week before publish.
  Multiple slots offered for follow-up questions, fact-check, and
  the call after first draft.
- **The founder will give a live demo on a physical iPhone** during
  the interview — this is the highest-conversion 5 minutes of the
  meeting. Do not skip it.
- **Fact-check pass:** the journalist sends quoted material for
  fact-check before publish. We turn it around in under 12 hours.
- **No surprises rule:** the journalist tells us the angle and the
  framing before publish. We do not preview copy and we will not
  ask to. We do reserve the right to walk away from a piece that
  changes angle without notice between interview and publish.

### Off-the-record rules

- **No off-the-record commentary on acquisition discussions.**
  Ever. Not in any meeting, not in any back-channel. We will not
  comment on whether we are in conversation with strategic
  acquirers; we will not comment on rumors; we will not comment
  on internal valuation. The story is the product and the
  category. The strategic premium is named openly in this kit; we
  have no off-the-record version of it.
- **On-record by default.** Unless the journalist explicitly says
  "this is off the record" and the founder explicitly says "I'll
  go off record on this question," everything is on record. The
  founder is trained on this.
- **Background notes** (technical detail, context not for direct
  quote) are offered freely and labeled as such in real time.

### Press cadence

- **Phase 1 (May 2026):** This kit shipped. First warm intros to
  tier-1 outlets begin within 7 days. Cold pitches at 14 days if
  warm intros do not land the meeting.
- **Phase 2 (Q3 2026):** V0.2 ship — Live Activity hero. Coordinated
  press around the Apple Health Featured submission; one-outlet
  exclusive offered to a tier-1 desk on a feature-length piece.
- **Phase 3 (Q4 2026 / Q1 2027):** Clinical RCT 12-week data
  readout. Pre-print live; press cycle around the methodological
  rigor and the early effect-size estimate.
- **Phase 4 (2027):** FDA De Novo filing submitted. Press cycle
  around the category claim (Psyche AI), the protocol adoption
  (other implementations of BIP), and the strategic premium.
