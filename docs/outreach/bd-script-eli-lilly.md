## Section 4 — The honest counter

### Lilly's strongest counter: "We're building this internally"

Lilly has invested heavily in internal digital infrastructure. The
Cove acquisition (2021), the LillyDirect launch (2024), and the
in-house data science build-out are all signals that Lilly's
default is to build, not buy.

**The honest response:**

"The internal-build framing has three failure modes specific to
behavioral interrupt:

(a) **Data corpus latency.** The behavioral model requires 18-24
months at consumer scale to mature. Lilly's user base is patient-
plus-clinician (LillyDirect telehealth), not pure consumer at the
behavioral-trigger frequency we need. Building means starting from
clinical-trial-derived behavioral hypotheses, which are weaker than
real-world consumer slip-event data.

(b) **Protocol-shape mismatch.** What Lilly would naturally build
is a vertical app integrated tightly into LillyDirect. What COYL
is, structurally, is a protocol-first runtime that's intentionally
horizontal. The horizontal shape is what makes the multi-indication
expansion possible. Lilly's natural shape doesn't.

(c) **Time-to-acquirer-blocking.** Novo's parallel conversation
exists. Lilly building means Lilly is racing to ship something
better than Novo's eventual acquisition of COYL. The race
calculation says: building takes 18-24 months; Novo can sign Path A
with COYL in 90 days and Path B in 12-18 months. Lilly cannot
out-build a competitor that's acquired the platform.

The right move is acquire. The cheaper move is acquire now (Path A,
$50-100M, optionality on Path B). The expensive move is wait until
Novo is in the room with a Path B LOI and we're negotiating against
a real bidder — that's the $4-6B conversation."

Don't oversell this argument. Lilly's CMO and Chief Strategy
Officer have heard variants of "you should buy us not build us" 100
times. The data corpus + protocol shape + acquirer-blocking
framing is what makes this specific case different.

### Lilly's secondary counter: "Why pay you over Noom Med, who's $1.5B?"

Real question. Noom is a public company; Noom Med is the
pharma-integrated layer. The valuation comparison is the explicit
question.

**The honest response:**

"Three differences:

(a) **Architecture.** Noom is a coaching program with weekly
cadence. COYL is a runtime interrupt that fires hundreds of times
per week per user. The architectural difference produces different
outcomes; the comparison isn't apples-to-apples.

(b) **Pharma-integration shape.** Noom Med's integration with
pharma is a content-and-coaching wrapper around the prescription.
COYL integrates as a runtime behavioral layer through the BIP
protocol — the integration is structural, not commercial. The
structural integration is what lets Lilly extract more value
(real-time clinical signal, multi-indication expansion, evidence
platform).

(c) **Price-of-control.** Noom is publicly traded; a Lilly-Noom
deal is a public-market negotiation. COYL is private; the
negotiation is bilateral; the price reflects strategic value to
Lilly, not public market expectations. The Noom comp ceiling is
the wrong frame. The right frame is: what does the parallel-bidder
Novo conversation produce, and what does Lilly lose by not being
in the parallel."

This counter is the most important one. Lilly's CFO will benchmark
against Noom. The Noom benchmark is the wrong benchmark. Walk Lilly
through why.
